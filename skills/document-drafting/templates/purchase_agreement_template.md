## 6. COVENANTS

6.1 **Pre-Closing Operations.** From the Effective Date until Closing, Seller shall:
(a) Operate the Business in the ordinary course consistent with past practice;
(b) Maintain the Purchased Assets in good condition;
(c) Not sell, transfer, or encumber any Purchased Assets without Buyer's consent;
(d) Maintain insurance coverage;
(e) Not enter into any material contracts without Buyer's consent;
(f) Provide Buyer reasonable access to premises, records, and personnel.

6.2 **Consents and Approvals.** Seller shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain all necessary third-party consents for assignment of contracts and transfer of licenses.

6.3 **Notification of Changes.** Seller shall promptly notify Buyer of any material adverse change, breach of representation, or condition that would make Closing impossible.

6.4 **Confidentiality.** Each Party shall maintain in confidence all non-public information disclosed during due diligence, except as required by law or with the other Party's consent.

(b) Own, manage, operate, control, or participate in any competitive business;
(c) Assist or advise any competitive business.

Exceptions: Seller may own up to {{PASSIVE_OWNERSHIP_THRESHOLD}}% of any publicly traded competitor.

7.2 **Non-Solicitation of Customers.** For {{NON_SOLICIT_CUSTOMERS_PERIOD}} following Closing, Seller shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or divert any customer of the Business.

7.3 **Non-Solicitation of Employees.** For {{NON_SOLICIT_EMPLOYEES_PERIOD}} following Closing, Seller shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit for employment or hire any employee of the Business.

7.4 **Enforcement.** Seller acknowledges that breach would cause irreparable harm and that Buyer is entitled to injunctive relief in addition to all other remedies.

(a) By mutual written consent;
(b) By either Party if Closing has not occurred by {{OUTSIDE_DATE}} (unless caused by terminating party's breach);
(c) By Buyer if any Closing condition in Section 8.1 is not satisfied;

10.2 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, this Agreement shall be void except for:

## JURISDICTION-SPECIFIC NOTES

**California:**

- Bus. & Prof. Code ยง 16600 limits non-compete enforceability to sale of business
- Non-competes must be limited to goodwill protection
- Bulk Sales Act (UCC Div. 6) may require creditor notice

**New York:**

- Non-competes must be reasonable in scope and necessary to protect legitimate business interests
- Consider successor liability under Labor Law ยง 220-b
- Sales tax clearance certificate recommended

**Delaware:**

- Business-asset sales not subject to bulk sales law
- Non-competes generally enforceable if reasonable
- Title insurance recommended for real property

**UK:**

- Non-competes enforceable if reasonable and protect legitimate business interests (goodwill, trade secrets)
- VAT implications - consider TOGC (Transfer of Going Concern) relief
- TUPE regulations may require employee transfer
- Stamp Duty Land Tax on real property transfer
