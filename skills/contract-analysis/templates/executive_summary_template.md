# Executive Summary Template

## Purpose

Generate a concise, stakeholder-friendly summary of contract analysis findings suitable for executives, managers, or non-legal team members.

## Structure

### Opening Statement (1-2 sentences)

Provide immediate context:

- Contract type and parties
- Overall risk assessment
- Key recommendation (sign/revise/reject)

**Examples:**

- "This Service Agreement between CompanyA and VendorB presents **MEDIUM RISK** with 3 missing critical clauses. Recommend revisions before execution."
- "The proposed NDA is **LOW RISK** and well-balanced. Recommend execution with minor clarifications."
- "This Employment Agreement contains **HIGH RISK** terms including an unenforceable non-compete. Recommend substantial renegotiation."

### Key Findings (3-5 bullet points)

Highlight most important discoveries:

- Critical missing clauses
- Significant risk exposure
- One-sided or unusual terms
- Major compliance concerns

**Format:**

- üî¥ [HIGH RISK finding with specific impact]
- üü° [MEDIUM RISK finding with specific impact]
- ‚úÖ [Positive finding or strength]

**Examples:**

- üî¥ **No liability cap**: Unlimited exposure for breach (could exceed $5M)
- üü° **30-day auto-renewal**: Contract extends automatically without opt-out notice
- ‚úÖ **Strong IP protection**: Clear ownership allocation with comprehensive licensing terms

### Business Impact (1 paragraph)

Translate legal risks into business consequences:

- Financial exposure
- Operational constraints
- Strategic implications
- Timeline considerations

**Example:**
"The unlimited liability clause exposes the company to potential losses far exceeding the $100K contract value. The 180-day notice requirement for termination prevents agile vendor management. However, the strong IP provisions ensure full ownership of deliverables, supporting the product development roadmap."

### Immediate Action Required (numbered list)

Specific next steps prioritized by urgency:

1. **[CRITICAL]** [Action with deadline]
2. **[IMPORTANT]** [Action with timeline]
3. **[RECOMMENDED]** [Optional improvement]

**Examples:**

1. **[CRITICAL]** Negotiate liability cap before execution (must-have term)
2. **[IMPORTANT]** Add force majeure clause covering pandemic/cyber events
3. **[RECOMMENDED]** Reduce termination notice from 180 to 90 days for flexibility

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's

‚úÖ Use plain English, avoid legalese
‚úÖ Quantify risks when possible ($amounts, percentages, timelines)
‚úÖ Lead with business impact, not legal theory
‚úÖ Provide specific, actionable recommendations
‚úÖ Use visual indicators (emojis, formatting) for scannability
‚úÖ Include confidence qualifiers when uncertain

### Don'ts

‚ùå Don't use legal jargon without explanation
‚ùå Don't list every minor issue (prioritize top 3-5)
‚ùå Don't provide vague recommendations like "review carefully"
‚ùå Don't make definitive legal conclusions (say "appears to" or "likely")
‚ùå Don't exceed 1 page for executive summary
‚ùå Don't hide critical risks in technical details

## Stakeholder-Specific Summaries

### For C-Suite / Board

Focus on:

- Strategic implications
- Financial exposure ($ amounts)
- Reputational risk
- Competitive impact
- Timeline and deal-breakers

### For Department Heads

Focus on:

- Operational constraints
- Resource requirements
- Timeline commitments
- Performance obligations
- Termination flexibility

### For Legal/Compliance

Focus on:

- Regulatory compliance
- Liability allocation
- Dispute resolution
- Jurisdiction issues
- Precedent concerns

### For Procurement/Finance

Focus on:

- Payment terms
- Financial liability
- Audit rights
- Pricing mechanisms
- Cost escalation

## Tone and Style

**Confident but Cautious**

- "This contract appears well-balanced..."
- "The termination clause likely provides sufficient protection..."
- "There is a high probability that..."

**Action-Oriented**

- "Negotiate a $500K liability cap before signing"
- "Request 90-day termination notice instead of 180 days"
- "Add force majeure clause covering cyber events"

**Business-Focused**

- "This saves the company $X annually"
- "Provides flexibility to switch vendors in 6 months"
- "Protects our competitive advantage through strong IP terms"

## Common Executive Questions - Prepare Answers

1. **"Can we sign this today?"**
   - Clear yes/no with 1-sentence reasoning

2. **"What's our worst-case exposure?"**
   - Specific $ amount or scenario

3. **"How does this compare to our standard agreement?"**
   - Highlight key deviations

4. **"What leverage do we have to negotiate?"**
   - Identify bargaining points

5. **"What happens if we don't sign?"**
   - Business consequences of walking away

6. **"How long will revisions take?"**
   - Realistic timeline estimate
